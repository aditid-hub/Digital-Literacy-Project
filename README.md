# Digital Literacy Ambassador Project
## Overview

** This repository presents the complete set of tasks completed as part of the Digital Literacy Ambassador coursework. Each task is aligned with a specific module and focuses on building essential digital skills, including awareness, professional presence, collaboration, communication, and cybersecurity.**

## Task Details
## Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

This task focuses on creating a visually engaging infographic to spread awareness about digital literacy among students. A design tool such as Canva or Google Slides was used to develop a one-page visual resource.

The infographic explains the meaning of digital literacy and highlights its importance in academic and professional life. It also covers key areas such as safe internet practices, useful digital tools for students, and maintaining a professional online presence. The aim was to present information in a concise and visually appealing manner so that it is easy to understand and share among peers.

## Task 2: Student Digital Portfolio

In this task, a foundational digital portfolio was created across multiple professional platforms. Accounts were set up or updated on platforms such as GitHub and LinkedIn.

The GitHub profile includes a structured README displaying basic academic details and learning goals. The LinkedIn profile was updated with current educational information to establish a professional identity. Additional platforms such as Kaggle or Stack Overflow may also be included to demonstrate engagement with technical communities.

This task emphasizes the importance of building a professional digital presence early and maintaining it consistently over time.

## Task 3: Coding and Collaboration Platforms

Part A: Coding Practice

A beginner-level coding challenge was completed on a platform such as HackerRank, CodeChef, or HackerEarth. This helped in developing problem-solving skills and gaining familiarity with coding environments. The completed challenge and any earned badge or score were documented.

Part B: Google Workspace Collaboration

A Google Form was created as a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz consisting of five questions, including both multiple-choice and short-answer formats. The form was designed to assess basic understanding of digital literacy concepts among peers.

A linked Google Sheet was used to collect and analyze responses, demonstrating the use of cloud-based collaboration tools for data collection and evaluation.

## Task 4: Professional Email and Etiquette Guide

Part A: Email Writing

Two professional emails were drafted:

A formal request to a professor for assignment deadline extension
An email expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity

Both emails follow proper structure, including a clear subject line, professional greeting, concise body, and appropriate closing.

Part B: Social Media Checklist

A checklist was created outlining responsible social media practices for college students. It includes clear do’s and don’ts to guide students in maintaining a positive and professional online presence.

This task highlights the importance of effective digital communication and etiquette in academic and professional settings.

## Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness
Part A: Case Study

A case study was developed on UPI/Online Payment Fraud, describing how such frauds occur, who is typically targeted, and the consequences faced by victims. The case is based on a real or realistic scenario to ensure practical relevance.

Part B: Prevention Checklist

A “Stay Safe Online” checklist was created with actionable tips to prevent cybercrime. It includes specific guidance on financial safety, especially related to UPI transactions, along with general digital security practices.

The checklist also includes official reporting channels such as the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline 1930.
