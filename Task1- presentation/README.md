# Infograph Description
For this task, I designed a one-page visual resource using Canva, leveraging its flexible layout tools to create a custom 3:4 ratio infographic. 
I chose a "Swimlane" and "Doodle" aesthetic, utilizing a vibrant pink-to-yellow gradient background to make the resource feel modern, approachable, and student-centric rather than overly academic. 
My design effectively synthesizes four critical areas of digital life: The Core (defining digital literacy), The Toolkit (essential software like Notion and Zotero), The Safety Net (security practices like MFA), and The Professional (email etiquette) ,Each section is contained within unique, hand-drawn style "callout" boxes to ensure high scannability for my batchmates.
One thing I found particularly interesting during the design process was the challenge of information hierarchy. 
It was a balancing act to fit three distinct topics plus a core definition into a single vertical space without it feeling cluttered. 
I discovered that by replacing long sentences with bold keywords and representative icons—like the coffee cup for professional presence or the shield for safety—I could communicate complex ideas instantly. This taught me that in digital advocacy, "less is more" when trying to capture a student's attention in a fast-paced digital environment.
